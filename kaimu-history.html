楷木雕刻 - 历史渊源 Camphor Wood Carving - Historical Origin

历史渊源

楷木雕刻起源于2500多年前的春秋时期，是孔子故里曲阜的传统工艺。相传孔子的弟子子贡为了纪念老师，用楷木雕刻了孔子及其夫人亓官氏的雕像，这是楷木雕刻的最早记载。

唐宋时期，楷木雕刻逐渐发展成为一种成熟的工艺，主要用于制作祭祀用品、佛像、文房四宝等。明清时期，楷木雕刻达到鼎盛，成为皇家贡品，工艺水平达到了前所未有的高度。

Historical Origin

Camphor Wood Carving originated from the Spring and Autumn Period more than 2,500 years ago and is a traditional craft in Qufu, the hometown of Confucius. According to legend, Zigong, a disciple of Confucius, carved statues of Confucius and his wife Qiguan Shi with camphor wood to commemorate his teacher, which is the earliest record of Camphor Wood Carving.

During the Tang and Song Dynasties, Camphor Wood Carving gradually developed into a mature craft, mainly used for making sacrificial utensils, Buddha statues, four treasures of the study, etc. During the Ming and Qing Dynasties, Camphor Wood Carving reached its peak and became a tribute to the imperial court, with the craft level reaching an unprecedented height.
