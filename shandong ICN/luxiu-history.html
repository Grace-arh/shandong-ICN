鲁绣 - 历史渊源 Lu Embroidery - Historical Origin

历史年限

2000多年

Over 2,000 years

历史渊源

鲁绣起源于春秋战国时期，是中国历史最悠久的刺绣工艺之一。早在《诗经》中就有关于鲁绣的记载，当时称为"齐纨鲁缟"。汉代时，鲁绣已经成为宫廷贡品，工艺达到很高水平。

唐宋时期，鲁绣进一步发展，吸收了江南刺绣的技艺，同时保持了北方粗犷豪放的风格。明清时期是鲁绣的鼎盛时期，出现了许多著名的绣坊和艺人。

鲁绣在历史上曾是重要的贸易商品，促进了齐鲁地区与外界的文化交流。如今，鲁绣已成为山东文化的一张名片，在国内外享有盛誉。

最早记载

"Qi Silk and Lu Gauze" in Book of Songs

Historical Origin

Lu Embroidery originated in the Spring and Autumn Period and Warring States Period, and is one of the oldest embroidery techniques in China. It was mentioned in the "Book of Songs" as "Qi Silk and Lu Gauze". During the Han Dynasty, Lu Embroidery became a tribute to the imperial court, with its craftsmanship reaching a high level.

During the Tang and Song Dynasties, Lu Embroidery further developed, absorbing techniques from Jiangnan embroidery while maintaining its northern bold and unconstrained style. The Ming and Qing Dynasties were the peak period of Lu Embroidery, with many famous embroidery workshops and artists emerging.

In history, Lu Embroidery was an important trade commodity, promoting cultural exchanges between Qilu region and the outside world. Today, Lu Embroidery has become a business card of Shandong culture and enjoys a high reputation at home and abroad.
